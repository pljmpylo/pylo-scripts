Install the app
Add to your home screen
This app runs entirely in your browser — no App Store needed. Follow these steps to install it on your phone like a real app, with its own icon on your home screen.
📱 iPhone — Safari
1
Open the link in Safari
Make sure you're using Safari — not Chrome, Firefox or another browser. The install feature only works in Safari on iPhone.
2
Tap the Share button
At the bottom of Safari, tap the Share icon — the square with an arrow pointing up. If the bottom bar is hidden, scroll up slightly.
3
Tap "Add to Home Screen"
Scroll down in the share sheet and tap "Add to Home Screen". You can rename the app or leave it as "Running × Pilates".
4
Tap "Add" — done ✓
The app icon appears on your home screen. Open it — it launches in full screen, without the Safari address bar, exactly like a native app.
🤖 Android — Chrome
1
Open the link in Chrome
Use Google Chrome on your Android phone.
2
Tap the menu (⋮)
Tap the three dots in the top-right corner of Chrome to open the menu.
3
Tap "Add to Home screen"
Select "Add to Home screen" or "Install app" depending on your version of Chrome. Confirm and the icon will appear.
✓ Your progress is saved automatically
The app saves your profile, session completions, feedbacks and weekly check-ins to your phone's local storage. Your data will be there the next time you open the app — even after closing it completely.

Calendar export
Add your programme to your calendar
Export all 10 weeks of sessions directly to your calendar — so your runs, Pilates and rest days appear automatically alongside the rest of your life.
Step 1 — Export the .ics file
1
Go to Programme → Download
Tap the "Download your programme" banner at the bottom of the Programme tab.
2
Set your start date and session time
Choose the date your programme begins and what time your sessions are scheduled by default (e.g. 07:00). A preview of the first 3 events will appear.
3
Tap "Export .ics file"
A file named alice-pilate-*.ics will download to your device. Each event includes the session title, description, duration, and a 30-minute reminder.
Step 2 — Import into your calendar
📱 Apple Calendar (iPhone / Mac)
iPhone: Open the Files app → find the .ics file → tap it → tap "Add All". All 10 weeks import in one go.
Mac: Double-click the .ics file — Calendar opens and asks which calendar to add events to. Select your calendar and click "OK".
🗓 Google Calendar
On desktop, go to calendar.google.com → click the ⚙ gear → Settings → Import & export → Import → select the .ics file → choose which calendar → Import.
Mobile: Google Calendar on iPhone/Android doesn't support direct .ics import. Use the desktop method above — events will sync automatically to your phone.
📧 Outlook
Open Outlook → File → Open & Export → Import/Export → Import an iCalendar (.ics) file → select the file → Import.
✓ What's included in each event
Session title and type · Phase of the programme · Full description · Session duration · A 30-minute reminder before each session.
💡 Tip: If you change your start date or race date later, re-export the .ics and re-import it. Delete the old events first to avoid duplicates.
Guide
How the app works
Everything you need to get the most out of your Running × Pilates programme.
The 4 tabs
Home
Your daily dashboard. Shows the current week, today's session, your race countdown, and quick stats. Set your race date here to align the programme to your goal.
Programme
Browse all 10 weeks. Check sessions off as done, leave feedback on each one, and export the full schedule to your calendar or as a PDF. Past days are automatically greyed out.
Today
Launch today's session. On a run day, choose between a full flow (body check-in + timed warm-up + run) or Quick Start (straight to the run). On rest or Pilates days, the options adapt accordingly.
Progress
Track how you've been feeling week by week. Session feedbacks are aggregated here — feeling levels, pain signals, averages. Complete weekly check-ins to log your overall condition.
Session types
Run Standard running session. Pace and duration increase gradually over the 10 weeks.
Long Run The weekly long effort, always at easy conversational pace. The cornerstone of endurance building.
Pilates Mobility and strength work. Complements running by targeting the stabilisers running wears down.
Rest Mandatory recovery. This is when your body adapts. Gentle stretching or a walk is fine.
Running flow
On a run day, you have two paths:
Body Check & Warm Up
3 quick check-in questions → 8 timed warm-up exercises (5 min) → run screen → post-run checkout (feeling, pain, energy). The checkout automatically marks the session as done and saves your feedback.
Quick Start
Shows the session objective and your paces, then takes you straight to the run screen — no check-in, no warm-up.
During the warm-up, "Next →" skips to the next exercise manually. "Start the run →" skips the rest of the warm-up entirely.
Feedback & tracking
Session feedback
After each session, log how it felt (feeling, pain, energy). Tap any session row in Programme to add or edit feedback. A coloured badge appears once feedback is saved.
Weekly check-in
Tap any week row in Progress to open the weekly check-in — 5 questions on pain, fatigue, motivation, sleep, and perceived progress. A verdict and personalised advice are generated based on your answers.
Progress dashboard
The "How you've been feeling" section in Progress aggregates all session feedbacks — good session count, pain signals, and average feeling per week.
Race date & programme alignment
Set your race date in Home or Settings. The app automatically aligns the programme:

More than 10 weeks away — the programme start date is calculated so you peak on race day.

Less than 10 weeks away — the programme starts at a later week to fit your timeline. This is ambitious; injury risk is higher with a compressed build.
Settings
Profile — change your name, switch programme (Road to 10K / Road to 21K), and update your 10K time or race date at any time.
Reset — erases all data — profile, sessions, feedbacks, check-ins and saved progress. The app restarts from the beginning.
Export — accessible from Programme → Download. Export all 10 weeks to your calendar (.ics) or as a printable HTML/PDF schedule.
